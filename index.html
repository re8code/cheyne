Gangseo-gu, SEOUL

Gangseo-gu is located in the western part of Seoul, bordering the Han River and close to Gimpo Airport.
With convenient transportation options like the Airport Railroad and Line 9,
it is an accessible area that has grown as a mix of new urban developments and residential spaces.
Historically, Gangseo-gu served as a transportation and trade center along the Han River
and has developed into a key urban area today.

HISTORY

An Important Base in the Goryeo Era

During the Goryeo period, Gangseo-gu was known as "Yangcheon-hyeon" and was a crucial military and trade area near the lower Han River. It played a significant role in facilitating trade and military movements.

Separation from Yangcheon-gu in the Joseon Dynasty

After the Joseon era, Gangseo-gu served as a strategic area protecting the capital,
and in 1977, it separated from Yangcheon-gu to form the present-day Gangseo-gu.

Urbanization and Development

Starting in the 1980s, as Seoul expanded, large residential complexes
and commercial zones around the airport were developed in Gangseo-gu.
This led to a significant population increase, transforming the area into a key hub in western Seoul.

LOCAL FOOD

Seolleongtang (Beef Bone Soup) 🥣 :

Gangseo-gu has many famous seolleongtang restaurants where you can enjoy rich and deep-flavored broth.

Naengmyeon (Cold Noodles) 🍜 :

Cold noodles, both in soup and spicy versions, are popular in the summer, with many renowned naengmyeon restaurants in the area.

Galbitang (Beef Rib Soup) 🍲 :

This hearty soup is served in generous portions in many restaurants across Gangseo-gu.

Dakhanmari (Whole Chicken Soup) 🍗 :

Known for its flavorful broth and tender chicken, many Dakhanmari spots in Gangseo-gu attract locals and visitors alike.

Beoseot Maeuntang (Spicy Mushroom Soup) 🍄 :

Known for its generous portions and bold, warming flavors, it’s a popular choice during colder months or for those craving a hearty, comforting meal.

FUN FACTS

✈ Home to Korea’s First International Airport ✈

Gangseo-gu houses Gimpo Airport, Korea’s first international airport,
which once served as the only gateway connecting Seoul to the world.

💧 Named "Gangseo (강서/江西)" despite Being West of the Han River 💧

The area is named Gangseo because it is on the western side of the Han River from Seoul’s perspective
and is known as a major residential area in western Seoul.

🌾 Seoul’s First Large-Scale Urban Agriculture Initiative 🌾

Gangseo-gu has a unique urban farming project, with programs around Yangcheon Hyanggyo
where residents participate in farming activities and educational experiences.
